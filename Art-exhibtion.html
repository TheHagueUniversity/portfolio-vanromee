Curatorial Rationale

I started my art project with making a mind map. I looked for items to weave and came up with the idea to combine food with normal kitchen utensils
and photograph this in a special situation. What remained one of the items throughout my work was the weaving pattern. As in every experimental
process accidents happen. During my experimenting with weaving patterns and ink, I had to remove ink spills. The effect of the ink spills on the
tissues gave me new inspiration. So I experimented further with tissues and ink. In the last part of my work I was inspired by my visit to the K20
museum in Dusseldorf which showed the works of Andreas Gursky. His works appealed very much to me because of the scale and techniques. Thus,
I tried to implement his idea but based on my interpretation. My artworks show a certain size combined with colour and certain dynamics.

My selection of works is based on three parts, which reflects the evolution which started with my first idea of using weaving. I started out weaving
in combining food and kitchen utensils seen in my work “Gevlochten Kaas”. The following three works was an experiment of weaving patterns using
ink and crayon. During the experimenting I was taken by the dynamics of the process which lead to the video art. Then the experimental accident
lead to a new inspiration for further artworks. In this second phase of my art project ink water and tissues were the bases of several experiments.
In experimenting with the effects of these materials I came up with very colourful and interesting visual results. It resulted in works that are more
three dimensional. My last pieces of artworks represent some larger items. In the work of Gursky, size, depth, and replication were a key. I used
those with my own twist. Andreas Gursky’s use of large scaled works and Chiharu Shiota’s use of lines played a role in influencing my style of work.
My works “Carla” and “Eerste” clearly show these influences and techniques.

I set up my exhibition in such a way that the viewer has to use more dimensions. Not only the straight view on the walls, but also up and down. I want
my viewers to really be inside the exhibition, and the exhibition goes around them. The second important aspect that I would like to transfer to the
visitor of the exhibition is the expression of colours. In my art project I used a lot of colourful materials, which are reflected in my artworks.
A further impression I would like to transfer to my audience is the dynamics I saw from the process of making my artwork. For this I used new digital
techniques like video and animated GIF. By making this repetitive it became art in itself. After being through this complete creative process of my art
exhibition, afterwards I can conclude that one of the main themes has been repetition. And I am very curious if my visitors to my exhibition come to
the same conclusions.
